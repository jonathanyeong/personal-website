I've been thinking about joy lately. Which is a super depressing sentence to write. I promise it's not that bad! It's more of a yearning for something new. It's wanting to change something drastic, like where I live or my career. It's hard not to compare yourself to others. To see what someone else has, and yearn for something similar. Whenever I have these existential crises I start to question my career choices. To get myself out of this mindset, I've found five things that bring me the most joy as a developer. If you're having a bad day or a similar existential crisis I hope this post sparks some joy in you!

### Community

Community is one of the biggest reasons I'm a developer. Being able to share experiences with people all over the world is incredible. If I wasn't in this industry, I don't know what my tribe would be. When I first started, it was only until I found the [CodeNewbie](https://www.codenewbie.org/) community that I felt like I made the right career choice. Another amazing community is the [DEV](https://dev.to/) community. It's inspiring to see people from around the world talk about their experiences with programming. The encouragement I get on that platform is what keeps pushing me to write about my experiences. There's still a long way to go in terms of diversity, equity, and inclusion. But having a community that includes people from all over the world, in all different disciplines makes me so happy.

### The AHA Moments

Do you ever get that feeling when you've spent hours on a problem that you just can't crack? It's like bashing your head against a brick wall. When this mental block happens to me, I go for a walk, maybe get some much needed sleep, or just have a good old dance party. Then when I come back to the problem the solution magically appears. This AHA moment is what I live for. One of the reasons I love being a developer is that you're put in these situations every day. You have to love solving problems, sometimes through code but a lot of times through communication. Most of the time it's about realizing where something goes or why someone called a method `do_the_things` (this actually happened to me).

### Building an idea

How cool is it to take an idea and realize it in the world? Now add in the fact that it may only take you weeks or months to get something up and running. Oh also, you can build everything by yourself, all you need is a computer. And you could probably do it lying on the beach (pending internet). That's a crazy thing to be able to do! One of the biggest things that drew me to being a developer was the ability to create beautiful websites. Something that brings joy to others. When I'm in the day to day grind, it's hard to see the big picture. But when I stop and reflect, I realize that I made something tangible by writing a few magic words. Mind. Blown.

### Privilege of Choice

I had initially wanted to talk about the ability to work from anywhere. But it's really about the privilege of choice. Being a developer grants you a lot of privileges that others don't. Lately, I've started trying to figure out how to help others with my privilege. To be transparent, diversity in tech is something I've only started thinking about when I moved to the States. Before that time I still felt like I was too new to be able to make a difference. And even now there's a lot more that I can do. But what I can do is to affect change to hiring processes within my company. I can be an active ally that speaks up to racial injustices. And be able to reassure others who are just starting out that they also have a voice. Having privilege as a developer and choosing to use that to help others is something I never want to stop doing.

I enjoy being a developer not because of the code. But because of the experiences around it. To this day, I feel like I need to prove that I'm passionate about writing code. It is and should be, totally fine to be happy with a paycheck and be able to use that to find fulfillment in other parts of life. But when I stop and think about my life as a developer. I realize that I'm happy with everything it's given me. I hope this list has brightened your day!
